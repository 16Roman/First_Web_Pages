Mobile Vs. Desktop

People's prefered way of searching the web has slowly shifted from desktops to mobile. In 2013, the majority of web traffic (82.98%) was coming from desktops. However, in November 2016, mobile web traffic surpassed desktop for the first time, with 50.7% of overall internet traffic coming from mobile phones and 49.3% from desktops.

As of May 2023, 67.81% of total web visits in the US are from mobile devices, while 32.19% come from desktops. The two platforms have been trading places over the past few years, with neither firmly establishing itself as the dominant web browsing device.

Information taken from Oberlo.com
